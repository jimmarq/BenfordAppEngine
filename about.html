About to Benford's Law

Benford's Law states that the distribution of leading digits in real life data sources is not even. In fact, the number 1 appears as the first digit in about a third of all numbers.

Benford's Law can be verified easily by looking at stock prices in the newspaper. Pick 50 random stocks, and see how many times the first digit is a 1. It will probably appear as the first digit about 16 times. If the numbers were evenly distributed, 1 would only show up as the first digit about 6 times.

Fraud Detection

Fraud detection is one of the interesting uses of Benford's Law. Suppose an employee is trying to steal money from his company by creating fake invoices. The employee will pick numbers that he thinks is random. Suppose he picks $945.16, $527.58, $838.92, $822.23, $283.84, $559.22, $1283.54 etc. Notice that the numbers he picked rarely started with a 1. Although the numbers appear to be random, Benford's Law would show that they may have been human generated.
